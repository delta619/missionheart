Our Cause

Congenital heart disease (CHD) is one of the most common contributing factors towards childhood morbidity and mortality. In India, approximately 3,00,000 babies are born every year with some form of CHD. Of these, 1,00,000 require surgical or trans catheter intervention to alleviate their symptoms or prolong their life or both. Given our current infrastructure, we are able to cater to about 20,000 babies while 80,000 children/year continue to suffer or perish due to their heart problem. There are multiple reasons for the treatment not being available to all and these include lack of awareness, inability to access primary health care, educational backwardness, cultural taboos and most importantly lack of funds. To add to these, girls get even more neglected due to gender bias in many communities.  CHD is not a single entity but comprises of a spectrum of malformations of the heart. At one end of the spectrum you have disorders which require multiple interventions while at the other end you have simple defects which can be corrected with a single procedure resulting in normal quality of life and longevity. Atrial septal defect, ventricular septal defect and patent ductus arteriosus are some of the defects which can almost be cured with timely interventions.

‘Mission Heart Foundation’ supports the cardiac treatment of pediatric patients with Coronary Heart Disease, this will include device closures as well as surgical repairs. We provide funding for the treatment for the financially challenged girl child. We will begin with covering children from Mumbai and its suburbs Navi Mumbai and plan to expand the activity to other parts of Maharashtra.
